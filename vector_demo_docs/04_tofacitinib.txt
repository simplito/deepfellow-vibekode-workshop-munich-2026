Tofacitinib (Xeljanz) — Summary of Product Characteristics

Pharmacotherapeutic group: selective immunosuppressants, Janus kinase (JAK) inhibitors.
Mechanism of action: Tofacitinib is a small-molecule, oral inhibitor of Janus kinases
(JAK). It inhibits primarily JAK1 and JAK3, and to a lesser extent JAK2. JAK kinases
transmit intracellular signals from the receptors of cytokines and growth factors
involved in haematopoiesis and immune cell function. By inhibiting the JAK-STAT pathway
tofacitinib modulates the inflammatory response. Unlike monoclonal antibodies it is not
a biologic drug but a synthetic molecule taken orally.

Therapeutic indications:
- Moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis (RA).
- Psoriatic arthritis.
- Ulcerative colitis.
- Juvenile idiopathic arthritis.
Tofacitinib is usually used in patients with an inadequate response to methotrexate or
biologic drugs.

Posology:
In rheumatoid and psoriatic arthritis, the standard is 5 mg twice daily orally, or 11 mg
once daily in the extended-release form. The dose is adjusted according to renal and
hepatic function and concomitant medicines.

Adverse reactions:
The most common are infections (including herpes zoster), headache, arterial
hypertension, and elevations of liver enzymes and lipid levels. The drug carries a
warning regarding an increased risk of serious cardiovascular events, thrombosis and
malignancies, especially in patients over 65 years of age.

Contraindications:
Active tuberculosis, severe infections, severe hepatic impairment, pregnancy and
breastfeeding.

Special warnings and precautions for use:
Before starting treatment, screening for tuberculosis and viral hepatitis should be
performed. During therapy, monitoring of the blood count, liver parameters and lipid
profile is recommended. Given the warnings concerning cardiovascular events and
thrombosis, the drug is used with caution in patients with risk factors.

Clinical efficacy:
In the ORAL trial programmes tofacitinib demonstrated efficacy in reducing disease
activity in rheumatoid and psoriatic arthritis, including in patients with an inadequate
response to biologic drugs. As an oral small molecule it provides an alternative to
injectable therapies, offering a different mechanism of action based on inhibition of
the intracellular JAK-STAT pathway.

Summary: Tofacitinib is an oral Janus kinase (JAK) inhibitor used in rheumatic and
inflammatory bowel diseases. It acts on the intracellular cytokine signalling pathway
rather than directly on a single extracellular cytokine.
