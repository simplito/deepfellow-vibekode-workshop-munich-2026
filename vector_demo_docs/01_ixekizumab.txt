Ixekizumab (Taltz) — Summary of Product Characteristics

Pharmacotherapeutic group: immunosuppressants, interleukin inhibitors.
Mechanism of action: Ixekizumab is a humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody that
selectively binds to the cytokine interleukin-17A (IL-17A) and neutralizes it.
Ixekizumab is an IL-17A antagonist. By blocking IL-17A it inhibits the release of
pro-inflammatory cytokines and chemokines, interrupting the inflammatory cascade
driven by the IL-17 pathway. Ixekizumab does not bind the ligands IL-17B, IL-17C,
IL-17D or IL-17F; its action is directed exclusively at IL-17A. It is a representative
example of a biologic drug that inhibits IL-17.

Therapeutic indications:
- Moderate to severe plaque psoriasis in adults who are candidates for systemic therapy.
- Psoriatic arthritis (PsA), as monotherapy or in combination with methotrexate.
- Axial spondyloarthritis, including ankylosing spondylitis.
The efficacy in these indications results from inhibition of the IL-17A axis, which
plays a key role in the pathogenesis of psoriasis.

Posology:
Plaque psoriasis: a starting dose of 160 mg (two 80 mg injections) at week 0, then
80 mg at weeks 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 and 12, followed by a maintenance dose of 80 mg every
4 weeks. The medicine is administered subcutaneously. After training the patient may
self-administer injections using a pre-filled pen.

Adverse reactions:
The most frequently reported are injection-site reactions and upper respiratory tract
infections (nasopharyngitis). Because IL-17A is involved in defence against pathogens,
inhibition of IL-17A by Ixekizumab increases the risk of fungal infections, especially
mucosal candidiasis. Cases of exacerbation of inflammatory bowel disease have also been
observed. Active tuberculosis must be ruled out before starting treatment.

Contraindications:
Hypersensitivity to the active substance, clinically important active infection (e.g.
active tuberculosis). Concomitant use of live vaccines is not recommended.

Special warnings and precautions for use:
The medicine should be used under the supervision of a physician experienced in the
diagnosis and treatment of conditions for which Ixekizumab is indicated. Because IL-17A
blockade affects mucosal immunity, patients should be monitored for signs of infection
during and after treatment. In the event of a serious infection, treatment should be
interrupted until it resolves. Particular caution is advised in patients with a history
of inflammatory bowel disease, as exacerbations related to inhibition of the IL-17
pathway have been reported.

Clinical efficacy:
In the pivotal UNCOVER trials a large proportion of patients treated with Ixekizumab
achieved a PASI 90 response, and many patients achieved complete clearance of psoriatic
lesions (PASI 100). These results confirm the central role of the cytokine IL-17A in
the pathogenesis of psoriasis and the high efficacy of biologic drugs targeting IL-17.

Summary: Ixekizumab is a modern biologic drug, a selective IL-17A antagonist, used
primarily in psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis. It belongs to the class of IL-17
inhibitors alongside secukinumab and bimekizumab.
