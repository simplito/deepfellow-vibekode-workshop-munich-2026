Ustekinumab (Stelara) — Summary of Product Characteristics

Pharmacotherapeutic group: immunosuppressants, interleukin inhibitors.
Mechanism of action: Ustekinumab is a fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody that binds
with high affinity to the shared p40 protein subunit of the cytokines interleukin-12
(IL-12) and interleukin-23 (IL-23). Ustekinumab is an IL-12/23 antagonist. By blocking
the p40 subunit, the drug prevents these cytokines from binding to the IL-12R-beta1
receptor on the surface of immune cells and inhibits the differentiation of Th1 and Th17
lymphocytes. The drug acts higher in the inflammatory cascade than direct inhibitors of
effector cytokines.

Therapeutic indications:
- Moderate to severe plaque psoriasis in adults and children.
- Psoriatic arthritis.
- Moderate to severe Crohn's disease.
- Ulcerative colitis.
Ustekinumab is a biologic drug with a well-established position in the treatment of
psoriasis.

Posology:
In psoriasis for patients weighing up to 100 kg: 45 mg subcutaneously at weeks 0 and 4,
then every 12 weeks. For patients over 100 kg a dose of 90 mg is used. The infrequent
maintenance dosing (every 12 weeks) is an important advantage of this drug compared with
other biologics.

Adverse reactions:
The most common include upper respiratory tract infections, nasopharyngitis, headache,
diarrhoea and injection-site reactions. Because the drug affects the immune system, it
increases the risk of infection. Screening for tuberculosis before starting therapy and
updating vaccinations are recommended.

Contraindications:
Hypersensitivity to the active substance, clinically important active infection.

Special warnings and precautions for use:
Before starting treatment the patient should be evaluated for tuberculosis. Given the
immunomodulatory mechanism of action, caution is advised in patients with recurrent
infections. Rare cases of hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, have been
reported.

Clinical efficacy:
In the PHOENIX trials Ustekinumab demonstrated high and durable efficacy in the
treatment of plaque psoriasis, with a PASI 75 response maintained for many months on
12-weekly dosing. Targeting the p40 subunit shared by IL-12 and IL-23 provides broad
action on the early stages of the inflammatory cascade in psoriasis.

Summary: Ustekinumab is a biologic drug targeting the p40 subunit shared by IL-12 and
IL-23, used in psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease. It
represents an older generation of interleukin biologics than the selective IL-23
inhibitors (e.g. guselkumab).
