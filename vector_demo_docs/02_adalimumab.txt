Adalimumab (Humira) — Summary of Product Characteristics

Pharmacotherapeutic group: immunosuppressants, tumour necrosis factor alpha (TNF-alpha)
inhibitors.
Mechanism of action: Adalimumab is a recombinant, fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody.
It binds specifically to tumour necrosis factor alpha (TNF-alpha) and neutralizes its
biological activity by blocking its interaction with the p55 and p75 cell-surface
receptors. Adalimumab is a TNF-alpha antagonist. TNF-alpha is a pro-inflammatory
cytokine of central importance in chronic inflammatory diseases. The drug does not act
on the interleukin pathway.

Therapeutic indications:
- Moderate to severe rheumatoid arthritis (RA).
- Moderate to severe plaque psoriasis in adults.
- Psoriatic arthritis.
- Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis.
- Ankylosing spondylitis.
Adalimumab is a biologic drug widely used in rheumatology, dermatology and
gastroenterology.

Posology:
In psoriasis: a starting dose of 80 mg subcutaneously, then 40 mg every other week,
beginning one week after the initial dose. In rheumatoid arthritis, typically 40 mg
every other week, often in combination with methotrexate. The drug is given by
subcutaneous injection using a pre-filled syringe or pen.

Adverse reactions:
The most common are infections (upper respiratory tract, sinusitis), injection-site
reactions, headache and musculoskeletal complaints. Inhibition of TNF-alpha increases
the risk of serious bacterial infections, reactivation of tuberculosis and reactivation
of hepatitis B. Screening for latent tuberculosis is mandatory before treatment.

Contraindications:
Hypersensitivity to the active substance, active tuberculosis or other severe
infections, moderate to severe heart failure (NYHA class III/IV).

Special warnings and precautions for use:
Before starting treatment and periodically during it, patients should be screened for
tuberculosis, including the latent form. TNF-alpha inhibition may mask the symptoms of
infection, so close monitoring is required. Caution should be exercised in patients with
demyelinating disorders and in those with a history of recurrent infections.

Clinical efficacy:
For over a decade Adalimumab has been a standard in the treatment of moderate and severe
plaque psoriasis, demonstrating significant improvement in the PASI score compared with
placebo. As a TNF-alpha inhibitor it acts broadly on the inflammatory cascade, which
explains its efficacy across many disease entities in rheumatology, dermatology and
gastroenterology.

Summary: Adalimumab is a biologic drug from the TNF-alpha inhibitor group, used among
others in psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis and RA. It belongs to the same family as
infliximab and etanercept.
